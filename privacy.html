Privacy Policy

For the current Readgate iPhone build. Last updated: April 10, 2026.

Readgate is designed to help users create a short reading pause before opening selected apps. This policy explains what the app stores, what it sends over the network, and how to contact us.

1. Information stored on your device

Readgate stores the following information locally on your device:

  • Your selected blocked apps.
  • Your onboarding and setup status.
  • Your reading session history, including article title, article URL, reading time, unlock count, and related usage statistics.
  • Shared local app state used by the iOS Family Controls extensions to hand off blocked apps into Readgate and keep the unlock window in sync.

2. Information sent over the network

When Readgate fetches a reading challenge, it requests article content from Wikipedia's public REST API. These requests are made from your device to Wikimedia servers and may include technical request data such as your IP address, user agent, and request timing under Wikimedia's own policies.

Readgate does not require account creation and does not operate its own analytics, advertising, or marketing tracking backend in the current app version.

3. Information we do not intentionally collect

  • No name, email address, or account profile is required to use the app.
  • No precise location data.
  • No contacts, photos, microphone recordings, or camera data.
  • No advertising identifier for cross-app tracking.

4. How information is used

Information stored by Readgate is used only to:

  • Remember which apps you want to gate behind a reading challenge.
  • Track whether you completed a reading session and whether a short unlock window is active.
  • Display local usage history and reading statistics inside the app.
  • Fetch article content needed to run the reading challenge.

5. Sharing

We do not sell your personal information. In the current app version, the app only communicates externally to retrieve article summaries from Wikipedia and related Wikimedia endpoints.

6. Retention and deletion

Data stored by the app remains on your device until you delete it by uninstalling the app or clearing the app's local data through device-level tools, if available.

7. Children's privacy

Readgate is not directed to children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children.

8. Changes

If Readgate's data practices change, this Privacy Policy will be updated and the effective date above will change accordingly.

9. Wikipedia content attribution

Reading challenge content is sourced from Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia. Wikipedia content is licensed under the Creative Commons Attribution-ShareAlike 3.0 Unported (CC BY-SA 3.0) license. Each article links to its original Wikipedia source. Readgate is not affiliated with or endorsed by the Wikimedia Foundation.

10. Contact

If you have privacy questions, contact support@readgateapp.com.
