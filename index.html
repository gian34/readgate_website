FAQ
Common questions.
Why does Readgate need Screen Time access?

Screen Time (Family Controls) is Apple's only API that lets third-party apps display a system-level shield when you open another app. Without it, Readgate can't intercept a blocked app at all.

Your Screen Time authorization is managed entirely by iOS and never shared with Readgate's servers.

Does Readgate collect personal data?

No account, no name, no email. Sessions and streaks live on your device. Readgate contacts Wikipedia for articles, Apple/RevenueCat for purchases, PostHog for anonymous product analytics, and Sentry for crash reporting.

I finished reading but the app is still blocked. Why?

If you have another Screen Time app installed (such as Opal or Freedom), it manages its own independent shield. Readgate can only remove its own block — it has no access to other apps' shields. Both need to be satisfied for the app to open.

Can I change which apps are blocked?

Yes. Open Readgate, go to Settings → Manage Apps & Websites, and use Apple's native picker to add or remove apps and websites at any time. Changes take effect immediately.

What's the difference between Free and Pro?

Free lets you protect 1 app or website with unlimited reading unlocks, a fixed 10-minute break window, and 7 days of reading history. Pro removes the app limit, lets you set your own daily unlock limit, customize the break duration from 1 to 60 minutes, and gives you your full reading history.

The shield doesn't appear when I open a blocked app.

Family Controls requires a physical iPhone running iOS 18 or later. The shield does not work in the iOS Simulator, on iPad, or on Mac.

Make sure Readgate is authorized: go to Settings → Screen Time → Readgate on your device and confirm access is enabled.
