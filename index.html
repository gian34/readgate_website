FAQ
Common questions

Can't find an answer? Contact support.

Why does Readgate need Screen Time access?

Screen Time (Family Controls) is Apple's only API that lets third-party apps display a system-level shield when you open another app. Without it, Readgate can't intercept a blocked app at all.

Your permission is stored entirely on-device. Readgate never communicates with Apple or any external server about your Family Controls authorization.

Does Readgate track me or send my data anywhere?

No. Your sessions, streak, and blocked app selection are stored locally on your device. The only outbound network requests are to Wikipedia's public API to fetch article content.

No account, no analytics, no backend server.

I finished reading but the app is still blocked. Why?

If you have another Screen Time app installed (such as Opal or Freedom), it manages its own independent shield. Readgate can only remove its own block — it has no access to other apps' shields.

Both apps need to be satisfied for the blocked app to open. If you rely primarily on Readgate, consider disabling shields in the other app.

How long is the unlock window?

10 minutes by default. Once the window expires, the shield re-activates automatically and the next time you open the blocked app requires a new reading session.

Can I change which apps are blocked?

Yes. Open Readgate, go to Settings → Manage Blocked Apps, and use Apple's native picker to add or remove apps at any time. Changes take effect immediately.

The shield doesn't appear when I open a blocked app.

Family Controls requires a physical iPhone running iOS 18 or later. The shield does not work in the iOS Simulator, on iPad, or on Mac.

Make sure Readgate is authorized: go to Settings → Screen Time → Readgate on your device and confirm access is enabled.

My streak disappeared. What happened?

Streaks are calculated from completed reading sessions stored locally on your device. If you reinstalled Readgate or cleared app data, the history is gone.

Streaks also reset if you miss a full calendar day without completing at least one article.

What happens if I close the article before finishing?

The session is abandoned and no unlock is granted. The blocked app stays blocked. A new reading session starts fresh the next time you open a blocked app.

Can I read more than one article per session?

Yes. After completing a challenge you'll see a "Read another article" button on the completion screen. Bonus reads count toward your streak but don't consume an additional unlock — your apps stay open.
