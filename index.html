The Circadian Rhythm

The circadian rhythm is a roughly 24-hour cycle in the physiological processes of living beings. Driven by a biological clock, it regulates sleep-wake cycles, hormone release, and body temperature.


In mammals, the central clock resides in the suprachiasmatic nucleus of the hypothalamus. Light detected by the eyes resets the clock daily, synchronising internal time with the external environment.
