How it works
Three steps to a healthier habit

No willpower needed. Readgate creates friction that actually pays you back.

1

Block your distractions

Choose which apps to block — Instagram, TikTok, YouTube, whatever steals your time. Uses Apple's built-in Screen Time framework, so it actually works. No VPN, no workarounds.

2

Read a short article

Every time you try to open a blocked app, Readgate presents a short Wikipedia article. Science, history, culture — always interesting, always short enough to finish.

3

Unlock & go

Finish reading and your apps unlock for 5 minutes. You get what you wanted — and you picked up something new along the way. Your streak keeps you coming back.

Why Readgate
Friction that
pays you back.

Other blockers just make you wait. Readgate makes you smarter every single day.

01

Built with Family Controls

Readgate uses Apple's Family Controls framework to apply system-level blocking on iPhone. The full flow is designed for a native build on a physical device, which is also the expected review path.

02

Real knowledge, every time

Reading challenges draw from curated Wikipedia-based topics with local fallbacks for launch. Science, history, art, technology, and culture stay short enough to finish before your unlock window begins.

03

Build a reading streak

Track how many articles you've read this week, your daily reading time, and your longest streak. Small daily habits compound fast — by the end of a month you'll have read dozens of articles.

04

Privacy by design

Most app data stays on your device. No account, no analytics, no ads, and no third-party SDKs. The only network requests go to Wikimedia endpoints to load article content. Wikipedia content is licensed under CC BY-SA 4.0.

05

Unlimited unlocks at launch

The current launch build does not impose a daily unlock cap. Finish the reading challenge, take your short break, and repeat whenever you choose to reopen a blocked app.

06

Quick iPhone setup

Onboarding walks through Family Controls access, blocked-app selection, and optional notifications. The support page documents the same path App Review needs to verify the experience.
